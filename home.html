Know me better

Nick Castillo

Graduating with a degree in Computer Engineering, Nick sought out a career in technology but was not sure exactly quite where he fit. Nick has a passion for developing code, working with people, and video/image producing, which puts him in quite a unique category.

Working at Brivo Labs introduced him to working with new technology involving Internet of Things devices. He first started managing a project that would allow a cell phone to unlock a door just by being in proximity of it using Estimote beacons. This later evolved by including a Nymi band. The Nymi band is a device that you put on your wrist and authenticates you by your ECG. The device also has BLE technology built in, so Nick created a solution that integrated the band, with the phone app, which uses the user's social profile to communicate with a BLE beacon, which then communicates with Brivo's backend to unlock a specified door.

Nick was able to accomplish all of this as he was given the resource of utilizing the crowd. He worked with co-pilots at Topcoder to create challenges where people all over the world would be able to compete and design/develop what he needed for the app he was building.

Currently, Nick is employed with Topcoder as a Developer Evangelist. He is currently working on creating several fun hackathons for Topcoder members that will take place all over the world. He is also coming up with ways to onboard new members in a fun and engaging way so that they become acclimated to the platform quickly.
